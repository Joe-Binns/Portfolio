Emotion Detection Model

The project explored the use of machine learning for emotion detection through facial recognition. It aimed to advance understanding of how to employ algorithms to interpret human emotions accurately from facial expressions. The project utilized the FER2013 dataset to train models capable of categorizing emotions, enhancing the way technology interfaces with users by offering a more intuitive digital experience.

The machine learning approach began with a RandomForestClassifier, yielding a baseline accuracy of 46.86%. After hyperparameter optimization with GridSearchCV, the accuracy slightly improved to 47.93%. Integrating PCA reduced accuracy to 45.27%, suggesting a loss of valuable features. A deep learning model implemented with Keras significantly improved the accuracy to approximately 81.16%, demonstrating the effectiveness of convolutional neural networks in handling complex emotional data.

Sklearn, Keras, Tensorflow, FER2013 dataset
